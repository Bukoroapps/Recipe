Buko Pandan

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 10 mins
Serving: 4

01.

Combine water and powdered gelatin then stir using a spoon

02.

Add Buko Pandan flavoring then stir until everything is evenly distributed.

03.

Heat a saucepan and pour-in the mixture. Bring to a boil while continuously stirring.

04.

Turn off the heat and transfer the mixture to a mold. Allow the temperature to cool. The texture of the mixture should be firm once cooled. You may also place this inside the refrigerator for faster results (allow the temperature to go down before putting-in the refrigerator).

05.

Combine condensed milk, table cream, sago pearls, and young coconut then mix well. Allow the texture to thicken by chilling in the refrigerator or freezer for a few hours.

06.

Slice the firm gelatin into 1 inch cubes then combine with the condensed milk-cream-young coconut-sago mixture..

07.

Transfer to individual serving platters or cups then top with a scoop of vanilla ice-cream.

Macaroni Fruit Salad

Prep: 5 mins
Cook: 10 mins
Serving: 4

01.

Cook macaroni according to package instructions. Discard water and arranged cooked macaroni in a mixing bowl. Set aside.

02.

Prepare the salad dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients in a bowl, including Lady’s Choice Mayonnaise. Mix until the texture smoothens. Set aside.

03.

Combine macaroni, fruit cocktail, kaong, and cheese. Gently toss.

04.

Pour the dressing mixture into the bowl. Toss until all ingredients are well blended.

05.

Cover the bowl. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour.

06.

6. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve for dessert. Share and enjoy!

Leche Flan

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 45 mins
Serving: 6

01.

Using all the eggs, separate the yolk from the egg white (only egg yolks will be used).

02.

Place the egg yolks in a big bowl then beat them using a fork or an egg beater

03.

Add the condensed milk and mix thoroughly

04.

Pour-in the fresh milk and Vanilla. Mix well

05.

Put the mold (llanera) on top of the stove and heat using low fire

06.

Put-in the granulated sugar on the mold and mix thoroughly until the solid sugar turns into liquid (caramel) having a light brown color. Note: Sometimes it is hard to find a Llanera (Traditional flan mold) depending on your location. I find it more convenient to use individual Round Pans in making leche flan.

07.

Spread the caramel (liquid sugar) evenly on the flat side of the mold

08.

Wait for 5 minutes then pour the egg yolk and milk mixture on the mold

09.

Cover the top of the mold using an Aluminum foil

10.

Steam the mold with egg and milk mixture for 30 to 35 minutes.

11.

After steaming, let the temperature cool down then refrigerate

Mayo Brownies

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 20 mins
Serving: 6

01.

Preheat oven to 350F.

02.

Prepare the dry ingredients by sifting flour and cocoa powder in a mixing bowl. Add baking powder and sugar. Mix well using a wire whisk. Set aside.

03.

On a separate bowl, crack eggs. Beat the eggs.

04.

Add Lady's Choice Mayonnaise. Mix well until all the ingredients in the bowl are well blended. Add vanilla extract and gradually add-in sugar. Continue to mix until texture becomes smooth.

05.

Melt the chocolate chips using a double boiler (watch video below for details).Stir-in the melted chocolate with the mayonnaise mixture. Mix well.

06.

Gradually add the dry ingredients with the mayonnaise mixture. Fold until smooth. Add chopped walnuts.

07.

Pour the brownie mixture into an 8x8 baking pan. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes.

08.

Remove from the oven. Let the brownies cool down.

Maja Blanca

Prep: 8 mins
Cook: 35 mins
Serving: 8

01.

Pour the coconut milk in a cooking pot and bring to a boil.

02.

Add the sugar, condensed milk, and whole sweet kernel corn then stir until all the ingredients are evenly distributed.

03.

Simmer for 8 minutes

04.

Combine the milk and cornstarch then whisk until the cornstarch is diluted

05.

Pour the fresh milk and cornstarch mixture in the cooking pot and stir thoroughly.

06.

Allow to cook while stirring until the mixture reaches your desired thickness

07.

Pour the mixture in a serving tray then arrange and flatten the top using a flat tool such as a wooden spatula

08.

Allow to cool down then refrigerate for at least 1 hour

09.

Garnish with toasted grated coconut (or latik if available)

Ube Halaya with Cheese

Prep: 5 mins
Cook: 45 mins
Serving: 4

01.

Pour coconut milk in a cooking pot. Let boil.

02.

Stir and add the condensed milk.

03.

Once the mixture starts to boil, add the grated purple yam and ube flavoring. Continue to cook in medium heat while continuously stirring for 30 minutes.

04.

Add butter. Stir. Cook until the mixture reaches your desired thickness. (I usually cook it for 10 to 12 minutes after adding butter).
