Pininyahang Manok

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 45 mins
Serving: 6

01.

Marinate the chicken in pineapple juice/concentrate (derived from the can of pineapple chunks) for 20 to 30 minutes

02.

Pour the cooking oil in a cooking pot / casserole then apply heat

03.

Sauté the garlic, onion, and tomatoes

04.

Put-in the chicken and cook until color of the outer part turns light brown

05.

Add the pineapple juice/concentrate marinade and fresh milk then bring to a boil

06.

Add the pineapple chunks and simmer until the chicken is tender and half of the liquid evaporates (about 20 to 30 minutes).

07.

Put-in the carrots and simmer for 5 minutes

08.

Add the bell pepper and fish sauce then simmer for 3 minutes

09.

Remove from the cooking pot / casserole and transfer to a serving dish.

Lechon Kawali

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 50 mins
Serving: 6

01.

Pour water on cooking pot. Boil

02.

Add the pork belly and 1 tablespoon of salt. Put some pepper and bay leaves. Boil for 30 minutes or until meat gets tender

03.

Remove the meat from the pot and let it cool down for a few minutes

04.

Spread 1 tablespoon of salt on the pork belly. Make sure to distribute it evenly on all sections

05.

Start to deep fry the boiled meat. Heat oil on a cooking pot.

06.

Put each piece of boiled pork belly into the hot oil with extra caution. Fry until crispy. Turn the meat over to completely fry the opposite side until crispy. Note: be extra careful when frying as oil can splatter. You can use the cover of the pot to cover it while oil splatters, but do not cover completely as steam will be trapped. It will cause more splatters.

07.

Remove the meat from the cooking pot. Arrange on a wire rack to cool down. Slice according to desired portions

Tokwa at Baboy Humba

Prep: 5 mins
Cook: 1hr 25 mins
Serving: 4

01.

Heat cooking oil in a wok or cooking pot. Add tofu. Deep fry until outer part turns golden brown and crisp. Remove from the wok. Set aside.

02.

Transfer 3 tablespoons cooking oil used to fry tofu on a clean pan. Heat and saute garlic until it starts to brown. Add onion. Continue sauteing until the later softens.

03.

Add pork. Cook until the outer part turns light brown.

04.

Pour soy sauce. Continue sauteing until liquid reduces to half.

05.

Add pineapple juice and vinegar. Cover and let boil.

06.

Stir and then add salted black beans. Cover once more. Continue cooking between low to medium heat for 35 minutes.

07.

Add water and brown sugar. Cook on a covered pan until pork gets very tender. Note: I usually cook it for 40 minutes in low heat.

08.

Add star anise. Cook for 5 minutes. Remove star anise from the wok.

09.

Season with ground black pepper and then add fried tofu. Stir and cook for 2 minutes.

10.

Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve.

Breakfast T-Bone Steak with Fried Eggs

Prep: 15 mins
Cook: 8 mins
Serving: 2

01.

Rub salt all over the steak. Let it stay for 15 minutes.

02.

Melt butter in a pan. Add olive oil.

03.

Gently pound the garlic until it cracks and then put it into the pan. Do not remove the skin. Note: this will give the steak a garlicky taste.

04.

Put the T-bone steak into the pan. Cook for 4 to 5 minutes between low to medium heat.

05.

Turn the steak over to cook the opposite side. Continue to cook for 3 minutes. You can also scoop some butter using a spoon and pour it on top of the steak while frying.

06.

Arrange on a serving plate. Season with ground black pepper

07.

Serve with fried eggs. Share and enjoy!

Ginataang Tilapia

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 25 mins
Serving: 3

01.

Heat the cooking oil in a cooking pot.

02.

Sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger in the hot oil

03.

Once the onion become soft, pour the coconut milk into the pot. Let boil and cover. Cook for 5 minutes between low to medium heat.

04.

Add the chili peppers and tilapia. Cover and simmer for 12 minutes.

05.

Add long green chili peppers and season with fish sauce and ground black pepper. Stir. Cook for 3 minutes.

06.

Add the spinach. Cover and cook for 1 minute.

07.

Transfer to a serving plate. Serve hot. Share and Enjoy!

Pork Menudo

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 1 hr
Serving: 6

01.

Combine pork, soysauce, and lemon in a bowl. Marinate for at least 1 hour.

02.

Heat oil in a pan

03.

Sauce garlic and onion.

04.

Add the marinated pork. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes.

05.

Pour in tomato sauce and water and then add the bay leaves.Let boil and simmer for 30 minutes to an hour depending on the toughness of the pork. Note: Add water as necessary.

06.

Add-in the liver and hot dogs.Cook for 5 minutes.

07.

Put-in potatoes, carrots, sugar,salt, and pepper. Stir and cook for 8 to 12 minutes.
